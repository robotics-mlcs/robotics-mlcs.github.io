MLCS Robotics Team

We are interested in developing algorithms that enable intelligent systems to execute complex real-world tasks and assist people in everyday life.

News

We are looking for highly self-motivated students to join our robot learning research. We are recruiting in particular for the two funded projects below.

NRF Project

Spatial Intelligence for Robotic Autonomy

We are developing a robot learning framework that spans the full pipeline — from high-level planning to low-level control.

See on Funding → Spatial Intelligence for Robotic Autonomy (NRF) IITP Project

XR Copilot for Construction

We are developing XR copilot technology based on non-verbal behavior for quality and safety assurance in the fabrication and installation of construction components.

See on Funding → Development of XR Copilot Technology (IITP)

What we expect

  • Able to work in person and full time for at least 6 months (40+ hrs/week during summer & winter; 20+ hrs/week otherwise).
  • Basic understanding of robot learning papers such as Diffusion Policy (DP), Action Chunking Transformer (ACT), and π0 (pi0).

How to apply

Send your CV, together with which project you would like to join, to both the professor and the robotics team.
